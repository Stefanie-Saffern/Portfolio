# Case Study: SolarEdge Go Mobile Application Guide

## Overview
SolarEdge Go is a mobile application that enables installers and field technicians to remotely manage PV sites, fleets, devices, and alerts. The product team needed a comprehensive, user-focused guide that would support installers in performing critical tasks such as site creation, device replacement, remote access, and troubleshooting.

I led the end-to-end development of the SolarEdge Go Mobile Application Guide — from kickoff and SME alignment through content architecture, step-by-step flow design, technical validation, and final production.

## The Challenge
SolarEdge Go introduced new capabilities that significantly changed installer workflows, including:
*   Remote access to inverters
*   Multi-site fleet management
*   Step-by-step site creation flows
*   Device replacement and registration
*   Alert filtering and troubleshooting

However, early internal drafts and SME notes were fragmented, inconsistent, and lacked a unified structure. Installers needed a clear, task-based guide that reflected real-world usage and reduced support load.

## My Role
**Technical Writer — Mobile App Documentation Lead**

I was responsible for:
*   Defining the documentation scope and structure
*   Translating product requirements into user-focused content
*   Designing step-by-step workflows and task sequences
*   Creating clear procedures for site creation, device replacement, and alert management
*   Collaborating with PMs, UX, R&D, and Support
*   Ensuring accuracy across multiple app versions and feature rollouts
*   Preparing the guide for publication in the SolarEdge Knowledge Center

## Process

### 1. Kickoff & Requirements Gathering
I met with product managers, UX designers, and R&D to understand:
*   Installer personas
*   Core app capabilities
*   Release timelines
*   Known pain points
*   Required task flows

I also reviewed internal Jira tickets, UX prototypes, and early builds of the app.

### 2. Information Architecture
I created a structured outline that grouped content by installer tasks, not app menus.
This included:
*   Getting Started
*   Fleet Management
*   Site Management
*   Device Management
*   User Permissions
*   Alerts & Troubleshooting
*   Remote Access

This task-based architecture aligned with how installers actually work.

### 3. Step-by-Step Flow Development
Using the app build and UX flows, I documented:
*   Site creation (3-step wizard)
*   Device addition and replacement
*   Alert filtering and troubleshooting
*   Monitoring performance
*   Remote access sessions

Each procedure included:
*   Preconditions
*   Sequential steps
*   Notes and warnings
*   Expected outcomes

This ensured clarity and reduced ambiguity.

### 4. SME Reviews & Technical Validation
I ran multiple review cycles with:
*   Product managers
*   R&D engineers
*   Support engineers
*   UX designers

This ensured accuracy across:
*   Permissions
*   Supported inverter versions
*   Remote access limitations
*   Device registration logic

### 5. Final Production
I prepared the guide for publication by:
*   Standardizing terminology
*   Ensuring consistency across tables, notes, and warnings
*   Formatting for the Knowledge Center
*   Validating links, screenshots, and diagrams
*   Coordinating with localization teams

The final guide was published as the official SolarEdge Go Mobile Application Guide.

## Impact
✔ Improved installer onboarding
Clear flows reduced confusion around site creation, device replacement, and alert management.
✔ Reduced support tickets
Support teams reported fewer “how-to” questions related to the new app.
✔ Faster feature adoption
Installers were able to use remote access and fleet management features more effectively.
✔ Scalable documentation foundation
The structure I created now supports future app releases and localization.
